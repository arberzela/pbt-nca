Abstract

Understanding how local interactions generate sustained complexity without collapsing into degenerate attractors—such as frozen equilibria or static noise—remains a fundamental challenge in artificial life. While differentiable multi-agent systems like Petri Dish Neural Cellular Automata (PD-NCA) exhibit self-organization purely from multi-agent competition, they are notoriously susceptible to collapse.

We introduce PBT-NCA, a meta-optimization framework that bypasses this brittleness by subjecting PD-NCAs to a non-stationary fitness signal rewarding novelty both within each substrate and across all of them. Driven purely by competitive evolutionary pressure, the system spontaneously generates a plethora of emergent lifelike phenomena with behavioral and structural novelty over extended horizons—a sign of true open-endedness.

The substrate autonomously discovers diverse morphological survival strategies: highly regular coordinated periodic movements; spore-like scattering; and symbiotic partitioning where stable macro-structures gradually encapsulate smaller ones. By actively avoiding monocultures and dead states, PBT-NCA maintains effective complexity precisely at the "edge of chaos".

Emergent Dynamics

Lifelike Phenomena from Competitive Pressure

Without any handcrafted targets, PBT-NCA spontaneously evolves a diverse ecosystem of structures—shooters, gliders, amoebas, colonies, and spaceships—each arising from pure multi-agent competition.

Meta-iter 230 · 7 NCAs

Amoeba

Fluid, shape-shifting macro-structures that migrate across the substrate with coordinated, differentiated cell behavior—mirroring motility-induced phase separation.

Meta-iter 20 · 3 NCAs

Shooter

Directed projectile emission from stable territorial clusters—analogous to glider-gun structures in classical CA literature.

Meta-iter 460 · 7 NCAs

Glider

Persistent traveling waves that self-replicate across the grid without a fixed template—a universal marker of open-ended CA systems.

Emerging · 7 NCAs

Colony

Distributed cluster networks that exhibit stable territorial partitioning while maintaining active intra-colony communication via cell exchange.

Meta-iter 370 · 3 NCAs

Ant Colony

Decentralized foraging-like behavior: agents follow stigmergic-style pheromone trails emerging purely from local NCA gradient dynamics.

Meta-iter 20 · 3 NCAs (Extended Search Space)

Spaceship / Motherboard

Highly structured, periodically replicating entities with internal substructure—reminiscent of von Neumann universal constructors but arising from gradient-based learning alone.

Method Overview

PBT-NCA Framework

A meta-optimization loop that transforms standard population-based training into an open-ended regime discovery engine by replacing stationary fitness with novelty-driven selection pressure operating at two timescales.

01

Rollout & Score

Each of the P = 30 worlds is rolled out for Tw inner steps. Agents update via gradient-based learning while competing on the shared grid. Trajectories are scored by the dual-novelty fitness.

02

Archive Update (FIFO)

Top-m behavioral descriptors—species occupancy statistics (μ, σ, δ, entropy, alive-mass change)—are appended to a bounded FIFO archive. Archive novelty is computed as k-NN distance (k = 8) in descriptor space.

03

DINOv2 Visual Diversity

Each frame is encoded by a frozen DINOv2 encoder. Per-world diversity is the median cosine distance to all other worlds at the same timestep, averaged over the rollout—rewarding novel morphology beyond what handcrafted descriptors capture.

04

Exploit–Explore Replacement

Every K meta-iterations, the lowest-fitness worlds are replaced by Lamarckian copies of elite parents: weights, optimizer state, and ecological context are inherited, then crossover, mutation, and Gaussian weight perturbation are applied.

Dual-Novelty Fitness
Fi = 𝒩i + 𝒟i
Archive novelty 𝒩 plus DINOv2 visual diversity 𝒟. Complementary signals: 𝒩 captures ecological behavior relative to history; 𝒟 captures visual morphology relative to current peers.
Archive Novelty (k-NN)
𝒩i = (1/k) Σj∈kNN ‖di − dj‖₂
Mean Euclidean distance to k = 8 nearest neighbors in the behavioral descriptor archive A. Descriptor d ∈ ℝ3(N+1)+2 encodes species occupancy dynamics.
Visual Diversity (DINOv2)
𝒟i = (1/L) Σℓ medianj≠i(1 − zi,ℓᵀ zj,ℓ)
Median cosine distance between world i's DINOv2 embeddings and all other worlds at the same sampled frame ℓ, averaged over L frames.
Effective Complexity (Edge of Chaos)
Ceff(t) = H(gridt) × (1 − LZ77(gridt))
Product of spatial entropy and incompressibility. Vanishes for pure order or pure noise; peaks only at the edge of chaos. PBT-NCA achieves Ceff ≈ 0.21 ± 0.05 throughout training.
